IYRC Programs

I'm currently a student of three programs at IYRC: Medicine and Research, Community Health Literacy, and HEART (Though I created a separate space to talk about HEART!). These programs function like a university course, with weekly discussions, guest speakers, and guidance from medical and undergraduate students on our projects. Over the past three months, I’ve chosen to work on a capstone and a research project.

June 2024 - Expected September 2024

Throughout the summer, I've learned how to take a patient history, interview several standardized patients, engage in ethical discussions, have conversations about narrative medicine, and study various systems of human anatomy. By the end of this, I’ll also be certified as a Community Health Literacy Worker for stroke, dementia, and health insurance through the Columbia University Neurology Department. Because, clearly, I thought cramming a med school curriculum into a summer was the best way to enjoy my “break”!

For the Medicine and Research Program, I will be working on a systematic review on the predictive value of early warning signs for severe dengue in adult patients. I'll also meta-analyze existing studies to clarify contradictory findings, particularly regarding the significance of abdominal pain and vomiting. Right now, I'm at the phase of writing my results. Because nothing says "summer fun" like diving deep into the world of dengue research and figuring out if that stomach ache is just a bad lunch or something much more dramatic!

For my capstone project, I'm developing and implementing a dengue prevention mobile application for my hometown community of Paombong, Bulacan. This app aims to boost community engagement in dengue prevention efforts and assess its acceptability among users. Ultimately, the project aims to identify barriers and facilitators to app usage, with the goal of creating an effective tool to reduce dengue incidence in the region. The app is all finished—I'm just seeking feedback before launching it this August. Because, apparently, I'm not busy enough with everything else, so why not save a town from dengue too?
