Surprise, I read!

My mom, a total bookworm, made it inevitable for me to become one too. Now, I'm also a book hoarder. And yes, reading books and buying books are two entirely separate hobbies of mine! One is for expanding my mind, the other is for expanding my bookshelf until it collapses.

I love all genres—fiction, literary, non-fiction, historical fiction, fantasy, you name it. But when I first read Haruki Murakami's "Kafka on the Shore," I really started dabbling in magical realism. Murakami's writing blends magical realism, existentialism, and deep psychological insight, which keeps me coming back for more. His prose is often characterized by its simplicity and clarity that makes complex themes accessible. This is juxtaposed with surreal and fantastical elements. For instance, in "Kafka on the Shore," the narrative seamlessly transitions between the real and the surreal, blurring the boundaries of reality and fiction. Murakami explores profound philosophical questions without being overly didactic or esoteric—like a philosopher who also knows how to party.
