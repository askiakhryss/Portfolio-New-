Neuroscience Research Program

During the summer of my 10th grade, I applied for the Science Mentorship Institute under the neuroscience track because it sounded cool. Little did I know, this involved weekly discussions about writing research, neuroscience-related research, and computational experiment tutorials! Fortunately, I got paired with a mentor. Because what's summer without diving into the complexities of the human brain and pretending to understand computational experiments, right?

June 2023 - July 2023

One of the longest yet fastest parts of my life. I had a blast throughout the weeks of the program! The mentors and teaching fellows were incredibly kind and courageous. It solidified my determination to pursue STEM as a career path and seek out more opportunities after the summer. Who knew that dissecting the mysteries of the human brain could be so much fun—and make time fly faster than a neuron firing?

I was paired with a mentor who is an undergrad from Barnard. We worked through the project alongside the weekly synchronous sessions of discussions and assignments, which felt like a university course. Plus, there was the challenge of time zones—America to Asia is quite the difference, practically worlds apart. I had to sacrifice lots of sleep, but it was all worth it. My mentor was definitely one of the best ever—very considerate and incredibly smart. Who needs sleep anyway when you're dissecting neuroscience with someone halfway around the world?

I worked on a review studying the potential of integrating imaging and genomic data for Glioblastoma Multiforme treatment. It also highlighted the importance of understanding the unique genetic alterations and tumor heterogeneity in each GBM patient, which can enable the development of targeted therapies and enhance personalized treatment planning. After finishing the writing, I was fortunate to be approved to present at the symposium! Because nothing says "fun summer" like diving into the complexities of brain tumors and preparing to present your findings to a room full of experts.
