Activation and Immunomodulatory Roles of TLR7 and TLR8

I collaborated with Mr. Gokul Ramadoss, a UCSF PhD student, for 13 weeks to craft a systematic review through the Polygence research program. As part of the program's new AI tool launch, PolyPilot, I was one of ten participants chosen to review and provide feedback. In return, I got full mentorship support and enough caffeine to fuel a small rocket!

March 2024 - June 2024

The research delves into how two specific proteins, TLR7 and TLR8, help the immune system recognize and fight viruses. These proteins detect single-stranded RNA from viruses, triggering the body's immune response. The study examines their roles in enhancing immune responses, making vaccines more effective, and their potential in treating autoimmune diseases and cancer. It explores how activating these proteins can boost the production of important immune molecules and cells, aiding in the development of better vaccines and therapies for various diseases.

The methodology involves a systematic review of studies from the past ten years. A comprehensive search was conducted using PubMed and Google Scholar with terms related to TLR7, TLR8, single-stranded RNA, and vaccine development.
